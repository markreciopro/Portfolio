Advanced Excel Project

Summary of Analysis

Based on the Master Billing sheet and the pivot tables, here are the key findings to present to Jon:

  • Low Gross Margins: The data shows that overall gross margins are lower than the target of 45%. Some vendors and countries even have negative gross margins, meaning the company is losing money on those calls.
  • Vendor Performance: Vendor 1 is a significant drag on profitability due to its billing increment of 6 seconds. Calls with this vendor frequently result in overbilling and a negative gross margin, making it a critical area to address.
  • Client Rates: The Clients Rates tab shows that many clients are being billed at the same flat rate, regardless of the country they are calling from or the specific vendor. This lack of differentiation is a major reason for the low or negative margins on certain calls.
  • Data Organization: The data was initially in separate monthly sheets, which made it difficult to get a complete picture. Consolidating the data into a single Master Billing sheet and creating a single, comprehensive pivot table was essential for a proper analysis.

Recommendations and Next Steps

Here are some recommendations to discuss with Jon and the team, outside of the direct questions he asked:

  • Renegotiate with Vendors: The most urgent action is to renegotiate rates with the low-margin vendors, especially Vendor 1. You should present the data showing that their billing model is causing significant losses. If negotiation isn't successful, it's a good idea to seek out alternative vendors.
  • Adjust Client Rates: To achieve the 45% gross margin target, it is necessary to adjust client rates. Instead of a flat rate, clients should be billed at a rate that reflects the true cost of the call, including the country and the vendor. You can propose a new tiered pricing model.
  • Automate with Configurable Inputs: The sheet can be improved by adding a section for "configurable increases" where Jon or anyone on the team can input desired rate increases (e.g., 7% and 20%) and have the sheet automatically calculate the new margins. This would eliminate the need for manual formula updates and allow for quick what-if analysis.
